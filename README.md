# Women-Sexual-and-Reproductive-Health

Project Overview.

This project focuses on the analysis of maternal health risk data using Microsoft Power BI to identify patterns, trends, and clinical indicators associated with maternal risk levels across different patient groups.


<img width="1090" height="610" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/acfbdb92-fef0-473f-ba62-a2a7e3db19d9" />


The dashboard was designed to transform raw healthcare data into meaningful insights through interactive visualizations and healthcare-focused data storytelling techniques. The goal of the project was to better understand maternal risk distribution and support data-informed healthcare interpretation.



The dataset used for this project was sourced from Kaggle and contains records from 1,205 patients, including variables such as:



* Age.

* Blood Sugar.

* Heart Rate.

* Body Mass Index-BMI.

* Body Temperature.

* Risk Level.

* Diabetes Status.

* Other maternal health indicators.



Using Power BI, the dataset was cleaned, transformed, analyzed, and visualized into an interactive healthcare dashboard.

# Key Findings from the Analysis.



## 1. Maternal Risk Distribution.



The dashboard analysis showed that approximately 39.3% of pregnancies in the dataset were classified as high-risk.



### Insight.



This highlights the importance of continuous prenatal monitoring and early identification of maternal health complications.



---



## 2. Blood Sugar Patterns.



Average blood sugar levels were noticeably higher among patients classified within the high-risk category compared to low-risk patients.



### Insight.



This pattern suggests that blood sugar monitoring may play an important role in maternal risk assessment and patient monitoring.



---



## 3. Diabetes & Risk Category.



Patients with preexisting diabetes appeared more likely to belong to the high-risk maternal category.



### Insight.



The visualization highlights the importance of chronic condition monitoring during pregnancy.



---



## 4. Heart Rate Trends.



The dashboard showed higher average heart rate values among high-risk pregnancies compared to low-risk pregnancies.



### Insight.



This demonstrates observable differences in maternal health indicators across risk categories.



---



## 5. Age Distribution of Patients.



The majority of patients fell within the 20–29 age range, with the 20–24 age group recording the highest patient count.

 

Age Group and the Number of Patients.




* Under 20     -             214 patients.

* 20–24        -             305 patients.

* 25–29        -             298 patients.

* 30–34        -             180 patients.

* 35–39        -              91 patients.

* 40 and above -             117 patients.



### Insight.



Most pregnancies represented in the dataset occurred among younger reproductive-age women.



---



## 6. BMI Trends Across Age Groups.



Average BMI values increased progressively across older age groups.



### Insight.



This pattern demonstrates age-related variation in maternal health indicators within the dataset.
